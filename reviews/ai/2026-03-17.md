## Executive Summary

March 17 was the cleanest **post-frontal persistence day** in this stretch.

The day’s high was effectively set in the **first hour of the climate day** by leftover nighttime warmth, then the rest of the session was spent in a colder, drier continental regime. Unlike a shock/cliff setup, this day did not require chasing a sudden break. The edge came from recognizing that **the cold regime was real, durable, and not going away**, so the low-side distribution needed to be shifted colder all day.

The two main market truths were:

- **High side:** basically solved early — `T73` was the right expression, and there was little reason to keep paying for warmer high brackets after the first part of the night
- **Low side:** the key edge was **YES on `T57`**, especially around midday when the market was still pricing warmer middle brackets too comfortably

This is an excellent training case because it teaches the opposite lesson from a volatility/cliff day:

- **Do not wait for drama when the regime already tells you the answer**
- persistent NNW flow, rising pressure, falling dew point, and zero CAPE can be enough to keep dragging the floor lower without a dramatic event
