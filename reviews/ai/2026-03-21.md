# Executive Summary

KMIA on 2026-03-21 was not another failed-pulse day. It was a clean spring rebound after an early cool floor: CLI settled the low at 61F at 06:52 LST, the day warmed steadily into an 81F CLI high at 14:04 LST, and the late afternoon/evening path never reopened the warm tail. The live SKF `mixed_uncertain` bucket looks wrong for this realized structure; the day belonged in a cleaner rebound-and-lock regime.

# Ground Truth

- **CLI settlement is truth:** high **81F at 2:04 PM LST**, low **61F at 6:52 AM LST**.
- **Sampled obs extrema:** low **60.8F at 06:45 LST**, high **80.6F at 13:10 LST**.
- The obs and CLI were tightly aligned on both endpoints, so this was a high-confidence settlement day even though the exact obs peak stayed a few tenths below the CLI high.

# Regime Assessment

## `overnight_residual_cooling`

The opening hours still carried the previous cool-background signature. KMIA spent the overnight mostly in the low/mid-60s with upper-50s dew points, NW-N flow, and a pressure recovery into sunrise. That combination is exactly why the low side locked early.

## `delayed_diurnal_rebound_with_afternoon_high_lock` (new regime warranted)

This is the key call for the day. After sunrise, KMIA did not produce a failed warm pulse like 3/20. Instead it climbed steadily: 62.6F at 07 LST, 68.0F by 08 LST, low-70s by 09 LST, mid-70s by 10 LST, then upper-70s/around-80 from late morning into afternoon. That is a coherent rebound regime with a later-afternoon lock, not a messy mixed bucket.

**SKF misclassification callout:** the live SKF proposal updated `mixed_uncertain`, but by late morning that label no longer matched the realized path. The regime that should have been active was **`delayed_diurnal_rebound_with_afternoon_high_lock`**, or at minimum a rebound-lock regime rather than `mixed_uncertain`.

## `evening_cooling_reversion`

Once the high printed, the back half of the day was straightforward. KMIA slid from the upper-70s into the upper-60s by late evening with no sign of renewed heat. That confirms the high-side lock and supports aggressive warm-tail removal after the official high.

# Phase Breakdown

## 00-07 LST: residual cool floor

KMIA opened near 64-66F, then drifted down to a sampled 60.8F floor at 06:45 LST. Dew points sat mostly between 55-59F. Pressure rose from roughly 1016.6 hPa toward 1019 hPa, and winds held generally northwesterly to northerly. The physical story was simple: leftover continental influence was still strong enough to solve the low early.

## 07-11 LST: clean rebound takes over

After the low locked, temperatures rose almost monotonically. By 08 LST the station had already reached 68F, by 09 LST it was 69.8-73.4F, and by 10 LST it was 73.4-75.9F. This was not a transient probe. It was a durable warm-up leg, which is why the live `mixed_uncertain` label should have been abandoned.

## 11-16 LST: afternoon high lock

Late morning into mid-afternoon was the settlement-defining window. Sampled observations reached 80.6F at 13:10 LST and CLI later settled 81F at 14:04 LST. After that, temperatures spent several hours roughly 78-80F with easterly flow and modest dew points. The station looked capped, not poised for a second hotter surge.

## 16-24 LST: orderly evening fade

From late afternoon onward, KMIA cooled from 78.8F toward 66F by 23 LST. There was no late heat run, no renewed instability in the path, and no evidence the earlier high lock should have been reopened.

- `low_locked_early` should sharply reduce sigma on the cold side once CLI/obs alignment appears near sunrise.

- `warm_tail_removed_after_lock` should be aggressive here because the day immediately transitioned into plateau and then cooling, not into renewed acceleration.

# Spatial Confirmation

Regional stations backed the KMIA story.

- Inland/western spots were much colder at dawn and warmer by afternoon: Richmond Heights **55/83**, Kendall-Tamiami **55/80**, Florida City **57/82**, Immokalee **53/86**.
- Nearby airports were close enough to validate the KMIA shape without being identical: Opa-locka **61/80**, Hollywood North Perry **62/82**, Fort Lauderdale Executive **61/79**.
- FAWN also fit the same air-mass evolution: **55.4F at 05:45 LST** and **78.3F at 14:15 LST**.

That spatial pattern says KMIA was marine-tempered, not anomalous. The whole region saw a cold dawn and a meaningful rebound; KMIA just expressed the moderated airport version of it.

# Ocean Influence

Buoy and coastal water temperatures were warm enough to moderate the coast but not so warm that they erased the overnight cool floor.

- Hollywood Beach buoy water ran **75.9-77.2F**.
- Virginia Key water ran **72.5-75.6F**.
- Manatee Bay ran **69.8-74.7F**.

That helps explain why inland sites overperformed KMIA in the afternoon while KMIA still managed an 81F official high: marine influence tempered the amplitude but did not prevent the rebound-lock regime from winning.

# Upper Air Analysis

Upper-air fields supported a dry, recoverable spring setup rather than a cloudy tropical one.

- 925 mb temperature ranged **12.8C to 17.1C**.
- 850 mb temperature ranged **7.0C to 13.2C**.
- 850 mb RH spanned **12% to 100%**, which is broad, but the realized surface path never behaved like a deep moist tropical regime.

The practical takeaway is that the airmass was cool enough to lock the dawn low, yet dry/mixable enough to permit a substantial daytime recovery once morning heating took over.
