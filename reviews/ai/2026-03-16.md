## Executive Summary

This was a **high-side overperformance day** that turned into a **low-side timing trap**.

The high settled at **87°F**, well above cooler consensus solutions, because KMIA and the nearby network held a warmer, stronger-heating regime through the afternoon than much of the model stack expected. The low then settled at **69°F**, but not through a smooth evening cooldown. Instead, the late climate-day window broke sharply as wind rotated WNW/NW, dew point collapsed, and the warm-middle low brackets failed in the final part of the session.

The main lesson is that the day should be remembered as:

- **High side:** models too cool for too long
- **Low side:** endpoint alone was not enough; the real edge was recognizing the **late break in regime and path**
- **Market structure:** `B86.5` was the cleanest high-side winner; `B74.5` was the major low-side trap; `B68.5` was the low-side winner once the break was underway
